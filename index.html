Your Complete Guide to Traveling from Canada to Turkey

Plan your trip with the latest travel, health, and cultural information.

Documentation Requirements

Canadian citizens need a valid passport and an e-Visa to enter Turkey. The e-Visa can be applied for online at evisa.gov.tr.

Health Considerations

Ensure routine vaccinations are up to date. Recommended vaccines include Hepatitis A and Typhoid. Always carry travel insurance with medical coverage.

Itinerary Planning

Turkey has hot summers and mild winters. The best times to visit are April–June and September–October for pleasant weather.

Flight Considerations

Direct flights from Toronto to Istanbul take about 10 hours. Major airlines include Turkish Airlines and Air Canada. Book early for the best fares.

Local Transportation

Use Istanbul’s metro, trams, or buses with an Istanbulkart. Domestic flights are affordable for long distances.

Destination Considerations

  • Language: Turkish
  • Religion: Mostly Muslim
  • Currency: Turkish Lira (TRY)
  • Exchange Rate: ~1 CAD ≈ 24 TRY (as of 2025)
  • Cultural Tips: Dress modestly when visiting mosques; remove shoes before entering.

Time Zone

Turkey is 7 hours ahead of Ontario (Eastern Time). When it’s 12:00 p.m. in Toronto, it’s 7:00 p.m. in Istanbul.

Top 5 Things to See & Do

  1. Explore Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque (Istanbul)
  2. Hot air balloon ride in Cappadocia
  3. Relax on the beaches of Antalya
  4. Visit Ephesus ruins
  5. See the travertine pools of Pamukkale

Travel Scams, Safety & Security

Beware of overpricing in tourist areas and unofficial taxis. Use ATMs inside banks and keep your passport in a secure place.
